History of the Museum

The Egyptian Museum, located in Cairo, is home to one of the world's most extensive collections of ancient Egyptian artifacts. Established in 1902, it houses over 120,000 items, including the treasures of Tutankhamun. The museum's origins trace back to the 19th century, when Egyptologists began collecting relics to preserve the country's rich heritage.

Designed in a neoclassical style, the building itself is a monument to Egypt's past, featuring grand halls and intricate architecture that echoes the pyramids and temples of old.

Key Exhibits

Tutankhamun's Treasures

Discover the golden mask, chariots, and jewelry from the tomb of the boy king. This exhibit showcases the opulence of the 18th Dynasty.

Royal Mummies

Explore the preserved remains of pharaohs like Ramses II and Hatshepsut, offering insights into ancient mummification techniques.

Everyday Life

From tools and pottery to cosmetics and games, this section reveals the daily lives of ancient Egyptians across all social classes.

Animal Mummies

Learn about the sacred animals worshipped in Egypt, including cats, birds, and crocodiles, mummified as offerings to the gods.

Plan Your Visit

The museum is open daily from 9 AM to 5 PM. Tickets are affordable, and guided tours are available in multiple languages. Located in Tahrir Square, it's easily accessible by metro or taxi. Don't miss the rooftop views of Cairo!

For the best experience, arrive early to avoid crowds and explore at your own pace.
